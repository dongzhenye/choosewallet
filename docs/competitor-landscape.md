# Competitor Landscape

This document captures the current reference points for “choose a wallet” experiences and related crypto wallet recommendation resources. It summarises their strengths, weaknesses, and how Choose Your Wallet can differentiate.

## bitcoin.org – “Choose Your Wallet”
- **URL**: https://bitcoin.org/en/choose-your-wallet
- **Focus**: Bitcoin-only wizard with filters for control, validation, transparency, fees, environment (desktop/mobile/hardware).
- **Strengths**: Trusted heritage site, clear checklists, direct official links, multilingual support.
- **Gaps**: Limited to Bitcoin network, dated UI, no automated phishing/link assurances, no community contribution workflow.
- **Takeaways**: Maintain approachable wizard flow but extend to multi-chain coverage and modern accessibility standards.

## ethereum.org – Wallet Finder
- **URL**: https://ethereum.org/wallets/find-wallet/
- **Focus**: Official Ethereum Foundation directory with filters for custody, features, countries, and platform support.
- **Strengths**: Strong SEO, multilingual content, ecosystem authority, broad Ethereum-compatible coverage.
- **Gaps**: Ethereum-centric, lacks recommendations for Bitcoin or other chains, no emphasis on phishing-safe verification.
- **Takeaways**: Mirror structured data strategy and internationalisation, while positioning Choose Your Wallet as neutral and chain-agnostic.

## WalletScrutiny.com
- **URL**: https://walletscrutiny.com
- **Focus**: Security audits validating open-source status and reproducible builds for Bitcoin wallets.
- **Strengths**: Deep security insights, independent positioning, actionable transparency tags.
- **Gaps**: No UX-based recommendations, minimal non-Bitcoin coverage, onboarding remains technical.
- **Takeaways**: Integrate their security signals (open-source, reproducible) into metadata so users see both usability and security scores.

## CryptoWisser – Crypto Wallets Directory
- **URL**: https://www.cryptowisser.com/crypto-wallets/
- **Focus**: Large directory with filters, multilingual page variants, latest news tie-ins.
- **Strengths**: Extensive catalogue, UI filters for device, custody, fees, audience reach via translations.
- **Gaps**: Ad-heavy experience, limited transparency around verification of outbound links, monetisation biases.
- **Takeaways**: Aim for distraction-free UI, focus on verified data sourcing, and include clear change history.

## BuyBitcoinWorldwide – Wallet Guides
- **URL**: https://buybitcoinworldwide.com/wallets/
- **Focus**: Editorial “best wallet” listicles, primarily hardware wallet recommendations, lead-gen for newsletter.
- **Strengths**: Strong SEO, opinionated rankings, beginner-friendly comparisons.
- **Gaps**: Bitcoin-centric, affiliate-driven, minimal interactivity.
- **Takeaways**: Provide objective, filter-first guidance rather than prescriptive listicles; highlight phishing-safe official links.

## Additional Mentions
- **WalletAdvisor**, **WalletCompass**, **WalletPilot** (various domains) – older or parked brand concepts with limited traction; demonstrate naming space competition.
- **Media Listicles** (Nerdwallet, CoinGecko wallets page, Bitcoin Market Journal) – high SERP presence but often gated behind paywall prompts or Cloudflare challenges, emphasising opportunity for open access content.

## Opportunity Summary
1. Deliver a neutral, multi-chain advisor with both usability and security metadata.
2. Maintain transparent verification (official URLs, DNS fingerprints, social/PGP references) to combat phishing.
3. Use structured data and lightweight UX to compete long-term for “choose a wallet” and related queries.
4. Encourage community contributions via open GitHub workflow, including wallet teams and security reviewers.
